Tomato Chickpea Curry recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 1 onion
  • 1 teaspoon mustard, can be replaced with mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric
  • a pinch of chilli flakes
  • a pinch of cinnamon
  • 1 can of coconut milk / 400 ml
  • 1 can of chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cane sugar
  • 3 cans of chickpeas
  • to serve: boiled rice, chopped cabbage or a piece
    of kohlrabi, optional coriander

Preparation:

  1. Dice the onion and heat the oil in the bottom of a large pan.
    Add onion, mustard and salt, fry over medium heat for 4-5 minutes.
    If you don't have mustard, read the tips.
  2. Add the cumin, turmeric, chilli and cinnamon to the fried onion.
    Stir, fry for several seconds and add coconut milk, tomatoes, soy
    sauce and sugar. Increase the heat to maximum and cook for
    5 - 8 minutes, until the sauce thickens slightly and becomes glossy.
  3. Add the drained chickpeas, reduce heat to medium and cook for
    5-6 minutes. Season to taste with salt or more cane sugar if needed.
    Serve with rice and your favorite vegetables that can be dipped in
    a creamy sauce.

Tips:

  • If you don't have mustard, omit it and add 1 teaspoon of mustard
    to the curry together with the soy sauce.
  • Black mustard will be best for this recipe, but ordinary yellow
    will be good too, then it is worth adding a little more of it.
  • The curry is still cooking without the lid, thanks to which the sauce
    has a chance to evaporate and acquire a buttery, creamy taste.
  • Longer frying of onions with mustard seeds allows to flavor the oil
    and soften the onion properly.
  • Thanks to this recipe, you will get a lot of aquafaba, i.e. brine from
    a jar or can. Under no circumstances do not pour it out, just use it
    to make chocolate mousse, mayonnaise or fluffy pancakes.
